TuneLab

Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 2026

TuneLab does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data. Full stop.

What we collect

Nothing. TuneLab has no backend, no database, no user accounts, and no analytics. We do not know who you are, where you are, or how you use the app.

Your tune data

All tunes you save are stored locally on your device using your phone's storage. This data never leaves your device and is not accessible to us.

AI features

If you choose to use AI enhancement, you provide your own API key from a third-party provider (currently Google Gemini). Your key is stored locally on your device. When you use AI features, your tune data is sent directly from your device to the AI provider — it does not pass through any TuneLab server because there is no TuneLab server.

Your use of AI features is governed by the privacy policy of the provider you choose (e.g. Google's privacy policy for Gemini).

Car database

TuneLab fetches a car list from a public GitHub repository on launch. This is a standard HTTPS request with no personal data attached.

Google Play

TuneLab is distributed via Google Play. Google collects install and crash data as part of their platform. This is governed by Google's privacy policy and is outside our control.

Open source

TuneLab is fully open source. You can inspect exactly what the app does at github.com/super-android/tunelab.

Contact

Questions? Join the Discord server linked in the app or open a GitHub issue.
